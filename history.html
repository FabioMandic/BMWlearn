The History of BMW

BMW has a rich history of producing high-performance vehicles. BMW Start of production 1923:

The BMW 3/15 PS, introduced in 1927, was BMW's first production car. It was essentially a licensed version of the British Austin Seven, reflecting BMWs early automotive ambitions. The car featured a 747 cc four-cylinder engine, producing 15 horsepower, hence the name "3/15 PS." It had a top speed of 75 km/h and was known for its affordability and reliability. The model marked BMWs transition from aircraft and motorcycle manufacturing to the automobile industry.

The BMW 3/20, introduced in 1932, was an evolution of the 3/15 PS with significant upgrades. It featured a larger 788 cc engine, delivering 20 horsepower and improving performance. Unlike its predecessor, the 3/20 was designed entirely in-house by BMW, signaling the company's growing expertise in car design. It also introduced independent front suspension, enhancing ride comfort and handling. The model came in several body styles, including saloon and convertible, catering to diverse customer preferences.

The BMW 328, launched in 1936, is one of BMWs most iconic pre-war sports cars. It featured a 2.0-liter six-cylinder engine with 80 horsepower, offering exceptional performance for its time. The lightweight and aerodynamic design made it a dominant force in motorsports, winning prestigious races like the Mille Miglia in 1940. The 328s engineering innovations included tubular frame construction and hemispherical combustion chambers. It had a top speed of around 150 km/h, appealing to both enthusiasts and racers.

The BMW 335, unveiled in 1939, was a luxurious grand tourer aimed at the high-end market. It was powered by a 3.5-liter six-cylinder engine producing 90 horsepower, making it one of the most powerful BMW cars of its era. The 335 was available as a saloon or convertible, offering elegant styling and premium comfort. It featured advanced technology, including hydraulic brakes and an all-synchromesh gearbox.
